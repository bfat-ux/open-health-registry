About OHR

What we are and why we exist.

A nonprofit infrastructure organization. We build the data layer that national health systems need but rarely have the capacity to build alone.

The problem

Digital health in low-resource settings fails at a predictable point: systems can't verify the people and places they're meant to connect. Is this pharmacist licensed? Does this clinic exist? Is this the same patient who visited last month?

Without reliable answers to those questions, e-prescriptions can't be safely routed, referrals go to facilities that have closed, and patient records accumulate duplicates that clinicians can't trust. The problem isn't a shortage of health software. It's a shortage of the foundational data layer that health software depends on.

What OHR builds

Open Health Registry builds and transfers that foundational layer: provider verification registries, facility databases, and patient identity resolution infrastructure. Each component is built on open standards — FHIR R4, OpenAPI, PostgreSQL — with no proprietary lock-in.

The operating model is Build-Operate-Transfer. We build the system, operate it through the transition period, and transfer ownership — code, data, and operational knowledge — to the national authority. The country's data stays sovereign. The methodology stays with OHR and gets applied to the next country.

How OHR is different

Most health informatics projects are designed to be maintained by the vendor indefinitely. OHR is designed to become unnecessary. We document for handover from day one, train counterpart institutions during the operate phase, and define success as the moment the national authority no longer needs us.

We also don't fabricate consensus or overstate status. As of April 2026, OHR has one active program (the Nigeria Pharmacy Registry), one regulatory conversation underway (PCN), and no external users yet. We're building carefully.

Current status

Honest accounting, April 2026: OHR is not yet incorporated. There is no board. No funder commitments have been made. The Nigeria Pharmacy Registry has 6,891 records and is in active development; no external users are live yet. A regulatory conversation with PCN was initiated April 27, 2026. The site reflects what is true, not what is planned.

Bernard Fatoye, MBBS, MPA

Founder, Open Health Registry

Nigerian-trained physician (MBBS, University of Ilorin) with a Master of Public Administration from Texas A&M University. Bernard works as a Senior Clinical Informatics Specialist in the United States and has spent his career at the intersection of medicine and health information systems. He founded OHR to build the data infrastructure that Nigerian and African health systems need to make digital health work in practice, not just in pilots.

OHR and HTA+ — his commercial healthcare technology advisory firm — are deliberately separate organizations. OHR is nonprofit infrastructure; HTA+ is for-profit advisory. The two do not cross-promote.
