Health data infrastructure

A national health trust layer, built to transfer.

We help countries establish provider verification, facility registries, and patient identity resolution — so digital health systems can interoperate safely. We build on open standards, operate through transition, and transfer ownership to national authorities.

How it works Our programs

What we build

Health systems can't interoperate when they can't answer basic questions: Is this provider licensed? Does this facility exist? Is this the right patient? OHR builds the infrastructure that lets those questions get answered reliably.

Provider verification

Structured registries of licensed health professionals — pharmacists, physicians, nurses — with graduated trust levels and evidence trails. Regulators set the rules; the system enforces them consistently.

Facility registries

Geolocated, categorised records of health facilities — verified through field visits, GPS coordinates, and photographs. The foundation for referral networks, supply chains, and population health planning.

Patient identity resolution

Matching patient records across disconnected systems using national identifiers, biometrics, and probabilistic linking — without centralising sensitive data beyond what regulators authorise.

Operating model

Build. Operate. Transfer.

We don't build systems countries depend on us to run indefinitely. Every engagement ends with the national authority holding the code, the data, and the operational knowledge.

01

Build

We design and build on open standards — FHIR R4, OpenAPI 3.1, PostgreSQL — with documentation written for handover from day one. No proprietary lock-in; everything portable.

02

Operate

We run the system through the transition period, training counterpart staff, refining workflows with the regulator, and validating data quality against official datasets.

03

Transfer

We transfer code, data, and operational responsibility cleanly to the national authority. The reusable platform and methodology remain with OHR; the country's data stays sovereign.
