Nigeria Pharmacy Registry

OHR's first national program — building a verified, FHIR-native registry of every dispensing pharmacy and licensed pharmacist in Nigeria.

6,891 Dispensing endpoints
37 States + FCT covered
FHIR R4 Interoperability standard
L0–L4 Validation ladder active

Why this program matters

Nigeria's new Electronic Pharmacy Regulations (2026) require safe, auditable electronic prescription routing, premises verification, and real-time pharmacist licensing checks. Without a trusted, national source of truth for pharmacies and pharmacists, these regulations cannot be implemented at scale.

Most existing lists are static, outdated, or not queryable by digital health systems. The result: e-prescriptions fail to reach the right location, regulators cannot enforce standards efficiently, and patients face unnecessary risk. The Nigeria Pharmacy Registry solves this by creating a live, standards-based directory that every e-pharmacy platform, hospital system, and regulator can rely on.

What the registry contains

  • Dispensing endpoints (pharmacies/premises) with verified physical location, licensing status, and contact details
  • Licensed pharmacists linked to their practicing premises
  • Geospatial data for mapping and referral routing
  • Validation Ladder status for every record (L0 → L4) so systems can filter by trust level

All data is exposed via FHIR R4 resources (Location, Practitioner, PractitionerRole) and a clean OpenAPI 3.1 interface.

How it works

This program applies OHR's full methodology in production:

  • Validation Ladder — Records start at L0 and climb with evidence: contact confirmation → GPS photo → PCN cross-reference → high-assurance audit.
  • Build–Operate–Transfer — We build and operate the system with counterpart staff, then transfer full ownership of code, data, and knowledge to the Pharmacy Council of Nigeria.
  • Open standards, no lock-in — PostgreSQL + PostGIS backend, FHIR-native, OpenStreetMap basemap. PCN will own and host the system after transfer.

Current status — Honest accounting, April 2026

  • 6,891 dispensing endpoints loaded and mapped (L0 baseline)
  • Regulatory conversation with the Pharmacy Council of Nigeria (PCN) formally initiated on April 27, 2026
  • Data model and APIs live in development environment
  • No external systems connected yet
  • First field verification pilots planned for Q3 2026

The registry is deliberately starting small and transparent so we can move fast and earn trust.

Validation ladder in the NPR

Every NPR record carries a validation level. Here is what each level means in the context of pharmacy verification.

L0
Mapped Pharmacy name and location ingested from a data source. Not independently verified. Most NPR records are currently here.
L1
Contact Confirmed Phone call or SMS to the listed contact number confirmed and logged. Pharmacy is reachable.
L2
Evidence Documented Field visit with GPS coordinates and timestamped photo. Physical premises confirmed at the listed address.
L3
PCN Verified Record cross-referenced against the PCN registration database. Licence number and registration status confirmed with the regulator.
L4
High-Assurance Reserved for controlled substance dispensing endpoints. Requires in-person audit or biometric confirmation.
